#### **Scam Alert**

Beware of scam calls and scam messages impersonating the Committee Against Profiteering (CAP). Should you receive any suspicious call or Email, do report to the Singapore Police Force. 

##### 1.   Scam calls/ emails / SMSes made under the guise of the CAP

The CAP will not send you any SMS or email asking you to click on any link or provide any information outside of the CAP website and the official CAP feedback form on FormSG. 

The CAP will not call you unless you have provided feedback through the official CAP feedback form on FormSG, or if you have requested a call-back.

If you do receive a call from the CAP, you may wish to verify the Response ID of your feedback, or when your call back request was made.

##### 2.   Scam visits made to businesses under the guise of the CAP

If you are approached by persons who are assisting the CAP, you should ask for information about their agency/organisation and official identification. You can also contact their agency/organisation using the official contact details to verify their identity.

##### 3. I am a member of public and have received an email / SMS from the CAP. How do I know that the email / SMS is genuine?

There will be an acknowledgement email and SMS to those who provide their feedback through the the official CAP feedback form on FormSG.

The CAP will only contact you via our official email address with the domain “@cap.gov.sg”, if follow-ups are required. If you had requested a call-back, the CAP will not ask for your personal details over the phone.

A representative from the People’s Association may also approach you for further clarifications. If approached, always
request for official identification.

##### 4. I am a business owner and have received an email / SMS from the CAP. How do I know that the email / SMS is genuine?

CAP will only contact you via our official email address with the domain “@cap.gov.sg”, if required.

Some businesses may receive emails from the Consumers Association of Singapore (CASE) via CASE’s official Email address
with the domain “@case.org.sg”. CASE will not ask for your personal details over the phone.

A representative from the People’s Association may also approach you for further clarifications. If approached, always
request for official identification.

Should you receive any suspicious call or Email, do report to the Singapore Police Force.
